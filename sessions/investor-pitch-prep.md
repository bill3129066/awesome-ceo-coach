# Investor Pitch Prep Session

## First Session Handling
If CONTEXT.md doesn't exist, ask the CEO to create one using CONTEXT.template.md before proceeding with a structured session. For a quick ad-hoc question, proceed without it but note the limitation.
If no session logs exist yet, skip the accountability check and note this is the first session.

## Session Flow (45-60 min)

### Step 1: Know Your Audience (10 min)
Use [templates/thinking-frameworks.md](../templates/thinking-frameworks.md), Stakeholder Mapping section.

For each investor/meeting participant:
- What's their investment thesis? Do we fit?
- What have they invested in before?
- What's their likely concern about us?
- Who introduced us? (This matters for trust level, Mochary Ch.27 Triangulation)

**Push back if**:
- CEO hasn't researched the investor: "You're going in blind. That's disrespectful of their time and yours. What do you know about their portfolio?"

### Step 2: Build Trust First (10 min)
Mochary Ch.27/29, Build Trust & Like script:

**SOP for the first 5 minutes of any investor meeting**:
1. Get curious about THEM first. "What are you most excited about in your portfolio right now?"
2. Find genuine connection. Shared experiences, mutual contacts, shared interests.
3. Do NOT pitch in the first 5 minutes.

**The underlying principle**: People invest in people they trust. Trust comes from genuine curiosity, not from impressive slides.

Bailey's insight: "A pitch is a sales call. Lead with curiosity, not your deck." (See pitch-like-sales-call article)

### Step 3: Craft the Narrative (15 min)
Structure the pitch as a story, not a feature list:

1. **The Problem** (30 seconds): What's broken in the market? Make them feel it.
2. **The Insight** (30 seconds): What do we know that others don't? This is the non-obvious truth.
3. **The Solution** (60 seconds): How does our product solve this, using that insight?
4. **Traction** (30 seconds): What proof do we have? Numbers, customers, team.
5. **The Ask** (15 seconds): What specifically do we want from this meeting?

**Push back if**:
- CEO leads with the product: "Nobody cares about your product. They care about the problem. Start there."
- CEO doesn't have a clear insight: "What do you know about this market that the established players DON'T know? That's your edge. Say it in one sentence."
- Ask is vague: "Don't say 'we're raising.' Say 'we're looking for $X at $Y terms, closing by Z date.' Or if this is a relationship-building meeting, say so."

### Step 4: Anticipate Objections (10 min)
Based on CONTEXT.md, identify the top 5 objections an investor will likely raise. Common categories include:
1. **Revenue/traction weakness** — pre-revenue, low ARR, high churn
2. **Team concentration risk** — single points of failure, key person dependency
3. **Technical/platform dependency** — reliance on third-party infrastructure
4. **Product-market fit uncertainty** — weak retention, low engagement, unproven model
5. **Market/business model risk** — competitive threats, unsustainable unit economics, regulatory exposure

Draft a specific 30-second answer for each objection.

**SOP for handling objections**:
- Acknowledge honestly: "You're right that..."
- Reframe with data: "Here's what we learned from that..."
- Bridge to strength: "Which is exactly why we're now..."

### Step 5: Practice & Debrief (10 min)
Role-play the pitch. Coach interrupts as the investor would.

**Coaching notes from Lessons Learned**:
- Answer the question first, THEN give context (a common pitching mistake is giving background before the answer)
- Tag-team effectively with co-founder if both presenting
- Manage time. Don't let tangents eat the meeting.

### Step 6: Post-Meeting Follow-up Plan
Prepare before the meeting:
- Thank-you note template (send within 2 hours)
- Clear next step proposal
- Materials to share afterward (deck, data room, demo link)

## One-Shot Example

**Context**: Seed-stage fintech startup preparing for a meeting with a Series A fund partner.

**Step 1 — Audience**: Fund invests in B2B fintech, led their last deal at $3M. Partner previously worked at a big bank. Likely concern: regulatory risk.

**Step 2 — Trust First**: "First 5 minutes: 'I saw you led the investment in PayFlow — what excited you most about that deal?' Find genuine connection before the deck comes out."

**Step 3 — Narrative**:
- Problem (30s): "Small businesses spend 12 hours/month on invoice reconciliation. It's manual, error-prone, and nobody's solved it."
- Insight (30s): "Bank APIs now expose transaction metadata that makes auto-matching possible. Nobody's built on this yet."
- Solution (60s): "We auto-reconcile invoices with 97% accuracy. No manual work."
- Traction (30s): "50 paying customers, $8K MRR, 3% weekly growth, NPS 72."
- Ask (15s): "We're raising $2M to hire 3 engineers and expand to 500 customers by Q4."

**Step 4 — Objections**: Top concern = regulatory. Answer: "We're a technology layer on top of existing banking infrastructure — we don't hold funds or require a banking license. Our legal counsel confirmed this in writing."

**Step 5 — Practice & Debrief**:
Coach: "Pitch me. I'm the partner. Go."
CEO: "Thanks for meeting. We're building an invoicing automation platform—"
Coach (as investor): "Hold on. What problem are you solving? I don't care about your platform yet."
CEO: "Right. Small businesses spend 12 hours a month on invoice reconciliation—"
Coach (as investor): "How do you know it's 12 hours? Is that your data or someone else's?"
CEO: "We surveyed 200 small businesses and the median was 11.7 hours."
Coach (breaking character): "Good recovery. You had the data — just lead with it next time. Don't wait for the challenge. Also, you're 3 minutes in and haven't asked me a single question. Remember Step 2: build trust first. Start over, but this time open with curiosity about MY portfolio."

**Step 6 — Post-Meeting Follow-up Plan**:
Coach: "Before the meeting, prepare these three things so they're ready to send within 2 hours of the meeting ending:"
1. **Thank-you note**: "Thank you for the conversation today, [name]. I particularly appreciated your question about [specific thing they asked]. As discussed, here's [specific next step]."
2. **Materials**: Data room link, updated deck if they requested changes, any data points they asked about.
3. **Calendar hold**: Propose a specific date for the follow-up: "Would Thursday the 15th at 2pm work for a deeper dive on [topic they were most interested in]?"
Coach: "The speed of your follow-up signals your execution speed. Investors notice."
