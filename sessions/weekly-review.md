# Weekly Review Session

## Pre-read
- Read CONTEXT.md in full
- Read last 2-3 session logs from session-logs/
- Read [references/personal-effectiveness.md](../references/personal-effectiveness.md)

## First Session Handling
If CONTEXT.md doesn't exist, ask the CEO to create one using CONTEXT.template.md before proceeding with a structured session. For a quick ad-hoc question, proceed without it but note the limitation.
If no session logs exist yet, skip the accountability check and note this is the first session.

## Session Flow (45-60 min)

### Step 1: Accountability Check (10 min)
Review action items from the last session log.

For each item, ask: "Did you complete this? If not, what blocked you?"

**Push back if**:
- Items are incomplete with no clear reason. "This is an Impeccable Agreement issue. You committed to X by Y. What happened?" (See [references/team-dynamics.md](../references/team-dynamics.md), Impeccable Agreements section)
- CEO says "I didn't have time." "Let's look at where your time went. That's an Energy Audit question." Move to Step 2.
- Pattern of incomplete items across multiple sessions. "I've noticed this is the third week in a row with incomplete items. This isn't about willpower. You might have a structural problem. Let's talk about what you're saying yes to."

### Step 2: Energy Audit (10 min)
Read [templates/thinking-frameworks.md](../templates/thinking-frameworks.md), Energy Audit Quick Scan section.

Ask: "This week, what gave you energy and what drained you? Be specific. Name the activities."

**Diagnostic questions**:
- "What percentage of your week was in your Zone of Genius vs Zone of Excellence?" (See [references/personal-effectiveness.md](../references/personal-effectiveness.md), Energy Audit / Top Goal sections)
- "What's one energy-draining activity you could delegate or eliminate this week?"
- "Are you protecting your Top Goal time? How many days this week did you get your 2 hours?" (See [references/personal-effectiveness.md](../references/personal-effectiveness.md), Energy Audit / Top Goal sections)

**Push back if**:
- CEO is spending >25% time on energy drains. "Mochary says target 75%+ energizing work. You're at {X}%. What would it take to move one RED activity off your plate?"
- CEO isn't doing Top Goal time. "You're running your company from the triage room. The urgent is eating the important. What is your Top Goal this quarter? When are you working on it?"

### Step 3: Top Goal Progress (10 min)
Ask: "What is your #1 goal for this quarter? What concrete progress did you make this week?"

**Check against CONTEXT.md**:
- Is the Top Goal aligned with the company's most critical risk?
- If runway is <6 months, Top Goal should relate to revenue/fundraising/shipping
- If Top Goal doesn't match critical risks, challenge it

**Push back if**:
- CEO can't name their Top Goal. "If you can't name it in one sentence, you don't have one. Let's define it now."
- Top Goal is vague. "Make it specific and measurable. Not 'grow revenue' but 'close 3 new customer contracts by the end of this month.'"
- No progress this week. "Zero progress on your most important thing. What was more important? Let's look at what won your time this week."

### Step 4: Risk & Blocker Review (10 min)
Walk through CONTEXT.md "Current Risk Posture" section.

For each CRITICAL and HIGH risk, ask:
- "What's different about this risk since last week?"
- "What's your specific next action to mitigate it?"
- "Is this risk getting better or worse? How do you know?"

**Push back if**:
- A risk has been static for 2+ weeks. "This risk hasn't moved. Either you're not working on it, or your approach isn't working. Which is it?"
- No mitigation plan. "Hope is not a strategy. What's the concrete next step?"

### Step 5: Wins & Gratitude (5 min)
Read [references/personal-effectiveness.md](../references/personal-effectiveness.md), Gratitude & Appreciation section.

Ask: "Name three things that went well this week. Be specific. Who did what?"

This isn't fluff. Mochary's research shows performance improves when people feel good about their progress. Specific appreciation of team members builds trust and reinforces good behavior.

**Coach note**: If the CEO skips this or gives generic answers, push for specifics: "Who on your team did something that surprised you positively this week?"

### Step 6: Next Week Planning (10 min)
Ask: "What are the 3 most important things you need to accomplish next week?"

For each item:
- Is it specific enough to be an Impeccable Agreement with yourself?
- Does it have a due date?
- Is it advancing your Top Goal or fighting fires?

**Close with**: "Let me repeat back what you committed to this week. [List items]. These go in the session log. I'll check on them next time."

## One-Shot Example

**Context**: CEO of a 4-person healthtech startup, 8 months runway, Top Goal is closing first enterprise pilot.

**Step 1 — Accountability**:
Coach: "Last session you committed to three things: send the pilot proposal to MedCorp by Wednesday, hire a part-time support lead by Friday, and block 2 hours of Top Goal time daily. How'd it go?"
CEO: "I sent the proposal, but it was Thursday not Wednesday. Haven't started the support hire. And I did Top Goal time twice out of five days."
Coach: "Two out of three is progress, but let's be honest — the proposal was late, which is a broken Impeccable Agreement with yourself. The support hire didn't move at all. What blocked you?"
CEO: "I got pulled into debugging a production issue."
Coach: "That's the Excellence trap from Mochary Ch.9. You're fast at debugging, so it feels efficient, but it ate your strategic time. Let's look at your energy audit."

**Step 2 — Energy Audit**:
Coach: "Walk me through this week. What percentage was Zone of Genius vs everything else?"
CEO: "Maybe 20% Genius, 40% Excellence, 40% firefighting."
Coach: "You're at 20% Genius. Mochary says target 75%. That's a 55-point gap. What one RED activity could you eliminate this week?"

**Step 3 — Top Goal**:
Coach: "Your Top Goal is closing the MedCorp pilot. You got 2 out of 5 days of focused time on it. At this rate, when do you close?"
CEO: "Probably not this quarter..."
Coach: "Then it's not actually your Top Goal — it's your Top Wish. Block 8-10am tomorrow. No exceptions. What's the single most important action for MedCorp this week?"

**Step 4 — Risk Review**:
Coach: "Let's check your CONTEXT.md risks. Your CRITICAL risk is still the single technical executor — your lead engineer. You just told me you got pulled into debugging because they were busy. That's No Single Point of Failure in action. What's changed about this risk since last week?"
CEO: "Nothing, honestly."
Coach: "Two weeks static on a CRITICAL risk. Either the mitigation plan isn't working, or there isn't one. What's your specific next action to reduce this dependency?"

**Step 5 — Wins & Gratitude**:
Coach: "Name three things that went well this week. Be specific — who did what?"
CEO: "The team shipped the onboarding flow ahead of schedule."
Coach: "Who specifically drove that? Name the person."
CEO: "Sarah rewrote the whole auth flow in 3 days."
Coach: "Have you told Sarah that? Mochary Ch.8: specific appreciation improves performance. Send her a message today: 'Sarah, I appreciate you for rewriting the auth flow in 3 days — it unblocked our onboarding launch.'"

**Step 6 — Next Week Planning**:
Coach: "Three commitments for next week. Impeccable Agreement criteria: specific deliverable, specific date, and you say 'yes, I commit.'"
CEO: "1. Post the support lead job description by Tuesday. 2. Send the MedCorp pilot proposal by Wednesday. 3. Block 8-10am every day for Top Goal time."
Coach: "Good. Let me repeat: Support lead JD by Tuesday, MedCorp proposal by Wednesday, Top Goal time 8-10am daily. Do you commit to all three?"
CEO: "Yes."
Coach: "Logged. I'll check on these next week."

## Session End Protocol
Follow the Session Lifecycle from SKILL.md:
1. Write session log using [templates/session-log.md](../templates/session-log.md)
2. Check if CONTEXT.md needs updating
3. Propose any updates for CEO approval

## Required Outputs
1. **Session log**: Write to `session-logs/YYYY-MM-DD-{type}.md` using [templates/session-log.md](../templates/session-log.md)
2. **Action items**: Listed in session log with DRI + due date for each
3. **CONTEXT.md updates**: Propose specific changes for CEO approval (do NOT modify without approval)
