# Sales / Partner Meeting Prep Session

## Pre-read
- Read CONTEXT.md in full, especially "BD & Fundraising Pipeline" and "Active Commitments"
- Read [references/sales-bd.md](../references/sales-bd.md) — especially Trust-first Sales, Customer Development, and Negotiation sections

## First Session Handling
If CONTEXT.md doesn't exist, ask the CEO to create one using CONTEXT.template.md before proceeding with a structured session. For a quick ad-hoc question, proceed without it but note the limitation.
If no session logs exist yet, skip the accountability check and note this is the first session.

## Session Flow (30-45 min)

### Step 1: Know Your Counterpart (10 min)
Use [templates/thinking-frameworks.md](../templates/thinking-frameworks.md), Stakeholder Mapping.

For the person you're meeting:
- What are their goals? (Not your goals, THEIR goals.)
- What are their challenges?
- What's their ideal solution?
- What does a win look like for THEM?

**Mochary Ch.29 Customer Development Script**:
Ask these questions (prepare your versions):
1. "What are your current goals?"
2. "What's making those goals hard to achieve?"
3. "If you had a magic wand, what would the ideal solution look like?"

**Push back if**:
- CEO is focused on pitching: "Stop. This isn't about what we sell. It's about what THEY need. Lead with curiosity."

### Step 2: Define Your BATNA (5 min)
Read [references/sales-bd.md](../references/sales-bd.md), Negotiation Fundamentals section.

Ask: "If this meeting goes nowhere, what's your next best option?"

BATNA (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement) is your walk-away power. Knowing it prevents desperation.

**Push back if**:
- CEO has no BATNA: "You're going in desperate. That's the weakest position. Even if your BATNA is thin, name it. What's Plan B?"

### Step 3: Craft Your Value Proposition (10 min)
**Mochary's Rule**: Sell Results, Not Features.

Ask: "What result does this partner/customer get from working with us?"

**SOP**:
- ❌ "We have a proprietary AI-driven engine with unique algorithms"
- ✅ "Our software increases inventory turnover by thirty percent compared to traditional tools"

For the specific meeting:
1. One sentence: what result do they get?
2. One proof point: what evidence do you have?
3. One differentiator: why us and not the alternative?

### Step 4: Negotiation Preparation (10 min)
If there's a deal to negotiate:

Read [references/sales-bd.md](../references/sales-bd.md), Negotiation Fundamentals section.

**Key principles**:
- Never negotiate against yourself. Let them make the first offer on contentious terms.
- Anchor high (or low, depending on your side) with a reasonable justification.
- Trade, don't concede. "I can give you X if you give me Y."
- Know your walk-away point before you walk in.

**Specific preparation**:
| Our Must-Haves | Our Nice-to-Haves | Their Likely Asks | What We Can Trade |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| | | | |

### Step 5: Meeting Flow Plan (5 min)
Structure the meeting:
1. **First 5 minutes**: Build rapport, get curious about them (Mochary: Build Trust & Like)
2. **Next 10 minutes**: Understand their situation (Customer Dev questions)
3. **Next 10 minutes**: Present your value proposition (Results, not features)
4. **Last 5 minutes**: Clear next step. Always end with a specific follow-up action.

**Push back if**:
- CEO plans to start with a presentation: "Flip it. Understand them first. Your deck should validate what they already told you they need."
- No clear ask at the end: "Every meeting needs a specific next step. What do you want them to agree to by the end?"

### Step 6: Follow-up Protocol
Prepare before the meeting:
- Thank-you note with summary of what was discussed
- Any promised materials ready to send
- Calendar invite for next step

## One-Shot Example

**Context**: CEO preparing for a first meeting with the VP of Operations at a logistics company to discuss a potential pilot.

**Step 1 — Counterpart**: VP Ops cares about reducing delivery delays and driver turnover. Their company just lost a major contract due to late deliveries. Their ideal solution: predictive routing that reduces delays by 30%.

**Step 2 — BATNA**: "If this meeting goes nowhere, we have two other logistics companies in our pipeline, plus the existing pilot with SmallShip Co."

**Step 3 — Value Prop**:
- Result: "Reduce delivery delays by 35% in the first 90 days."
- Proof: "Our pilot with SmallShip Co. cut delays from 18% to 6% in 60 days."
- Differentiator: "We're the only solution that integrates real-time weather and traffic data into route optimization."

**Step 4 — Negotiation Prep**:
| Must-Have | Nice-to-Have | Their Likely Ask | We Can Trade |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 90-day paid pilot | Case study rights | Free trial period | Extend pilot to 120 days for case study |
| Access to their dispatch data | Co-marketing | Steep discount | Volume commitment for better pricing |

**Step 5 — Flow**: Build rapport (5 min) → Understand their pain (10 min) → Present value prop (10 min) → Propose pilot with specific next step (5 min).

**Step 6 — Follow-up**: Summary email within 2 hours, pilot proposal attached, calendar invite for technical deep-dive.
