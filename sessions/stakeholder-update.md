## First Session Handling
If CONTEXT.md doesn't exist, ask the CEO to create one using CONTEXT.template.md before proceeding with a structured session. For a quick ad-hoc question, proceed without it but note the limitation.
If no session logs exist yet, skip the accountability check and note this is the first session.

## Session Flow (30-45 min)

### Step 1: Identify the Audience (5 min)
Ask: "Who is this update for? What do they care about most?"

**Stakeholder types and their priorities**:
- **Investors**: Return potential, risk management, runway, milestones
- **Partners**: Technical capability, timeline reliability, mutual value
- **Team**: Strategic direction, execution priorities, and blockers
- **Board/advisors**: Strategic direction, what they can help with, blockers

### Step 2: Identify the Narrative (10 min)
Every update tells a story. Ask: "What's the 3-sentence version of where we are?"

**SOP for narrative construction**:
1. Where we were (last update)
2. What happened (progress + setbacks. Be honest.)
3. Where we're going (next milestones + asks)

**Push back if**:
- CEO only mentions good news: "You're burying the bad news. Stakeholders who discover hidden problems lose trust permanently. What's the hardest thing to report?"
- CEO is too negative: "Balance. Start with genuine progress, then address challenges with clear mitigation plans."

### Step 3: Draft the Update (15 min)
Use [templates/stakeholder-update.md](../templates/stakeholder-update.md) as the structure.

Coach the CEO through each section:
- **TL;DR**: Force to 3 bullets max. "If they read nothing else, what must they know?"
- **Highlights**: Specific, quantified achievements
- **Challenges**: Honest + mitigation plan for each
- **Metrics**: Only include metrics that are trending or meaningful, not vanity
- **Asks**: Be specific. "Intro to X" not "help with partnerships"

### Step 4: Anticipate Questions (10 min)
For the specific audience, predict their top 3 questions:

Ask: "If you were [stakeholder], what would worry you most after reading this?"

Prepare answers for:
- The hard questions they'll definitely ask
- The question you hope they DON'T ask (prepare for this one especially)

### Step 5: Review Tone and Ask (5 min)
Read through the draft one more time:
- Is the tone appropriate for this stakeholder?
- Is there exactly ONE clear ask? (Multiple asks = no ask)
- Does it build toward the next conversation, not close the door?

## One-Shot Example

**Context**: Pre-seed startup sending monthly update to angel investors after 3 months of operations.

**Step 1 — Audience**: Angels care about: milestones hit, runway, and specific asks for help.

**Step 2 — Narrative**: "3 sentences: Last month we were building the MVP. This month we shipped it and got 3 design partners on board, but our conversion from trial to paid is lower than expected. Next month we're focused on improving onboarding to fix conversion."

**Step 3 — Draft** (using template):
- TL;DR: Shipped MVP, 3 design partners, conversion needs work
- Highlights: MVP shipped 1 week ahead of schedule, 3 design partners signed
- Challenges: Trial-to-paid conversion at 8% (target: 20%), onboarding flow too complex
- Asks: "Intro to a UX researcher who specializes in B2B onboarding"

**Step 4 — Anticipate Questions**:
Coach: "You're an angel investor reading this. What's your first question?"
CEO: "Probably 'why is conversion only 8%?'"
Coach: "Good. Prepare a 30-second answer: 'Our onboarding requires 7 steps. We've already redesigned it to 3 steps based on user session recordings. We expect conversion to improve to 15%+ within 30 days of shipping the new flow next week.'"
Coach: "Now — what's the question you HOPE they don't ask?"
CEO: "Whether we're going to make it to Series A with this runway."
Coach: "Prepare for that one especially. Answer: 'At current burn, we have 14 months of runway. If conversion hits 15%, we'll be at $8K MRR growing 20% monthly, which puts us in a strong position to raise by month 10. If conversion doesn't improve, we'll cut scope and extend to 18 months.' Never dodge the runway question — own it with a plan."

**Step 5 — Tone & Ask Review**:
Coach: "Read through the draft one more time. Is there exactly ONE clear ask?"
CEO: "Yes — the UX researcher intro."
Coach: "Good. Multiple asks equal no ask. Does this update build toward your next conversation?"
CEO: "I think so — I mention the onboarding redesign shipping next week."
Coach: "Perfect. That gives them something concrete to follow up on. Send it."
